DeltaMath

DeltaMath was a world apart from my past experience. Before accepting the job, I had a long conversation with my favorite math teacher from highschool, who raved about DeltaMath and was very excited at the prospect of knowing someone there. Other than my boss and the company's founder, I was the only software developer there when I was first hired. We already had a working product with millions of users, so my first projects were focused on making internal business tasks easier and automated.

Quil Health

My first job out of college was at a health tech "startup" called Quil. It was a cool expierience, and a great way to ease into working in a remote-first environment while most companies were still struggling to adjust to the mid-COVID difficulties of operating a large workforce. I got to work closely with a contracted dev team from Monterrey, Mexico to quickly build and scale our product, while also reporting to well-established principle engineers from Comcast and Independance Blue Cross -- the two companies sponsoring the project.

Ultimately though, the product idea just wasn't great. Most people seemed either confused or ambivalent about why people would ever use the technology we were creating, which didn't feel like a great environment to be in.

NPSV

One year in college, I took a class called Human Genome Analysis. I found the topic facinating, and ended up working for the professor who taught the course over the summer that same year. The project I worked on was a continuation of his work at Mount Sinai. We created an ML model to predict the genotypes of structural variants in data from short-read next-generation genomic sequencing. You can read about it here.
